About Auntie Midwife

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is among the top technological trends that are expected to shape the future of teaching and learning.
Chatbots, or conversational agents, are software that can interact with a human user turn by turn using natural language,
and they are considered an exemplar utilization of AI and Machine Learning (ML) in healthcare.


Despite the increased interest in healthcare applications of chatbots, there is a lack of chatbot integration into formal learning settings.
This project introduces the case of a Conversational Virtual Assistant (CVA) prototype for healthcare decision-making skills regarding
antenatal care in pregnant women.


In contrast to typical virtual patients, who rely on predefined, multiple-choice input, the proposed CVA employs
Natural Language Processing and ML techniques to allow women to formulate their own utterances and interact with
the virtual patient in natural language.


In view of employing participatory design and co-creating open access chatbots for healthcare curricula, in order to familiarize stakeholders
with chatbot technology and facilitate the demand elicitation process, this CVA prototype will serve as the foundation for the co-creation sessions.
The CVA prototype's next steps in co-development are explored.



Takeaway

Having children brings happiness and it is a great blessing when both the mother and child survive. Pregnant women are among the most
vulnerable patients since they are more susceptible to infections and have limited mobility owing to their medical condition.
Furthermore, preventive measures such as social isolation have hampered their access to healthcare. Pregnancy can be scary especially
if you are a first-time mother-to-be.Naturally, you will have numerous questions: what you should be eating,
if symptoms you are experiencing are harmful to the baby, what vitamins you should be taking, etc.
Reduced anxiety is critical for the health of the baby during pregnancy. In this age of false information, unreliable data can be harmful
to pregnant women during prenatal and postnatal periods. Having a medium to reach out to someone to talk with, can help minimize worries
and provide answers to questions, hence the introduction of a chatbot system to support pregnant women from the comfort of their own homes.
